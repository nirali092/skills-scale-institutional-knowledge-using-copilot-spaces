# OctoAcme Project Management Documentation

Welcome to the OctoAcme Project Management process documentation. This folder contains comprehensive guides for managing projects across all phases of the project lifecycle—from initial concept validation through execution, release, and continuous improvement.

## Quick Overview of OctoAcme Project Management Processes

OctoAcme follows a structured, lifecycle-based approach to project management grounded in five core principles: **customer-first delivery**, **iterative development**, **clear ownership**, **data-informed decisions**, and **psychological safety**. 

The organization's project lifecycle spans five distinct phases:

1. **Initiation** — Validate business need and stakeholder alignment by producing a lightweight One-pager that captures the problem statement, measurable success metrics, and initial timeline.

2. **Planning** — Break work into shippable increments with clear acceptance criteria, map dependencies, and establish a Definition of Done to ensure execution efforts are focused and measurable.

3. **Execution** — Deliver features through a defined team rhythm (daily standups, weekly delivery syncs, sprint-based planning) with clear role ownership, quality gates (unit tests, integration tests, security scanning), and CI/CD automation.

4. **Release** — Deploy to production following a standardized checklist that includes staging smoke tests, security verification, and rollback plans to reduce risk and improve observability.

5. **Close & Retrospective** — Capture learnings through structured retrospectives, convert them into tracked action items, and drive continuous improvement across the team.

**Key to success** is active risk management, structured stakeholder communication (weekly syncs, status templates, clear escalation paths), and role-based accountability with designated Project Managers, Product Managers, Developers, and QA teams collaborating to deliver on commitments efficiently.
